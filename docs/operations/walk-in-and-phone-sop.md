# Walk-In and Phone Triage SOP

**Purpose:** Define how Isaac handles walk-in patients and inbound phone calls when there is no front desk or receptionist. Applies from day 1 through any period of solo operation.

## Guiding Principles

- Safety first. Any potential emergency is redirected to 911 or the ER immediately.
- You cannot see walk-ins mid-appointment. Your existing patient comes first.
- Declining a walk-in is not refusing care — it is managing capacity honestly.
- Every interaction, including declines, gets a brief note (paper or digital).

---

## 1. Walk-In Triage

### 1.1 When you are between appointments (available)

1. Greet the walk-in at the front.
2. Ask: "Are you experiencing anything that feels like an emergency right now — chest pain, difficulty breathing, or something else urgent?"
   - If YES → go to §1.4 (Emergency Redirect).
3. Ask what brings them in today.
4. Assess whether you can accommodate:
   - You have at least 30 minutes before your next scheduled patient.
   - The presenting concern is within your scope of practice.
   - The patient is an existing member OR you are willing to create a new patient record today.
5. If you can accommodate: proceed with normal check-in (see [`day-1-clinic-runbook.md`](./day-1-clinic-runbook.md) §2).
6. If you cannot accommodate: go to §1.3 (Scheduling Later).

### 1.2 When you are with a patient (unavailable)

DO excuse yourself briefly from the exam room only if needed. Otherwise, use a posted sign at the front:

> "With a patient. Please call (210) 891-9005 or text to leave a message. I'll be with you as soon as I'm free."

When you are free:
1. Check if the walk-in is still present.
2. If yes: proceed per §1.1.
3. If they left without leaving contact info: log the date and time of the attempted visit. No further action required.

### 1.3 Declining or Deferring a Walk-In

Use this script verbatim or adapt naturally:

> "I appreciate you coming in. I'm not able to take unscheduled visits today [or: 'right now'], but I'd like to get you booked for the soonest available time. Can I get your name and phone number? I'll text you a booking link, or you can schedule at hollis.health."

Steps:
1. Take name and phone number.
2. Text them the scheduling link within 15 minutes of the interaction.
3. Log the walk-in attempt in your daily notes: date, time, first name, reason if stated, outcome (deferred/scheduled).

DO NOT apologize for the clinic's existence or imply they have been wronged. You are managing a clinical schedule safely.

### 1.4 Emergency Redirect

If the patient shows or describes any of the following, redirect immediately. Do not attempt to treat on site.

**Redirect triggers:**
- Chest pain or pressure
- Difficulty breathing
- Altered mental status or confusion
- Stroke symptoms (facial droop, arm weakness, slurred speech)
- Severe allergic reaction
- Active bleeding that is not controlled
- Any symptom the patient describes as "the worst [pain/symptom] of my life"
- Suicidal ideation with plan or intent

**Script:**

> "What you're describing sounds like it needs immediate emergency care. Please call 911 now, or I can call for you. Do not drive yourself. Go to the nearest emergency room."

If you call 911 on their behalf, stay with them until emergency services arrive or someone else takes over.

DO NOT attempt to treat a true emergency in the clinic. DO NOT delay the 911 call to assess further.

After the patient leaves: document the interaction in your daily log (no PHI in plain text unless inside the secure system). If the patient is an existing member, note the referral in their chart.

## 2. Inbound Phone Triage

### 2.1 When to answer

- Answer if you are between patients and the call takes less than 5 minutes to resolve.
- Let calls go to voicemail if you are in an exam room. Returning calls within 2 hours during clinic hours is the standard.
- After hours: calls go to voicemail automatically. See [`after-hours-messaging-sop.md`](./after-hours-messaging-sop.md).

### 2.2 Call opener

> "Hollis Health, this is Isaac. How can I help you?"

### 2.3 Scheduling a new patient

> "I'd be happy to get you scheduled. The easiest way is to book at hollis.health — you can see real-time availability there. If you'd prefer I walk you through it, what days and times generally work for you?"

Then open the web-admin calendar and create the appointment, or direct them to the patient-facing booking flow.

### 2.4 Existing patient clinical question

- If the question is simple and answerable safely in under 2 minutes: answer it.
- If the question requires chart review or a longer conversation: "Let me pull up your chart and call you back within [timeframe]. Is this number the best one?"
- Log a note in the patient's chart that you returned the call and what was discussed.

DO NOT give clinical advice that requires chart review before you have reviewed the chart.

### 2.5 Caller requesting to speak with a doctor who is not available

There is no other doctor. You are the clinician.

> "I'm the clinician here. I'm with a patient right now but I'll call you back within [timeframe]. Can I get your name and best number?"

### 2.6 Angry or difficult caller

- Stay calm. Do not match their tone.
- Acknowledge: "I hear that you're frustrated."
- Redirect: "I want to help. Here's what I can do: [specific action]."
- If the call becomes abusive: "I need to end this call now, but I'm happy to help when we can speak calmly. You can reach me by email at isaac@hollis.health." Then end the call.
- Log the call in your daily notes without including PHI in plain text.

### 2.7 Emergency call

If a caller describes an emergency:

> "Please hang up and call 911 immediately. I cannot provide emergency services over the phone. Call 911."

Stay on the line only if they refuse to call 911 and are in immediate danger, and in that case call 911 yourself from another line or device.

---

## 3. Record-Keeping

Every walk-in and every returned call involving clinical content gets a brief log entry:
- Date and time
- First name (or "unknown walk-in")
- Presenting concern (general, e.g., "joint pain") — no detailed PHI outside the secure system
- Outcome (scheduled, deferred, emergency redirect, voicemail left)

Keep this log in a dated section of your daily paper notes or as a draft note in the web-admin messaging system.
